# Discogs Migration — Project Context

## Design Context

### Users
Experienced Discogs collectors with 1,000+ release libraries who need to split, merge, or reorganize collections across accounts. These are power users — technically comfortable, detail-oriented, and deeply invested in their collection's accuracy. They run this tool locally on macOS, deliberately, not casually.

**Job to be done:** Execute high-stakes collection migrations with confidence — knowing exactly what will move, where it will go, and what happened if something goes wrong.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Technical. Focused.**

A no-nonsense power tool built for people who know exactly what they want. Dense with capability, rewarding to master. Think professional audio equipment: serious, purposeful, built to last.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference:** Discogs itself — music-forward, slightly editorial. Typography that nods to print and liner notes.
- **Theme:** Dark with warm undertones (navy, near-black). Warm orange and sage green accents.
- **Feel:** Heavy, intentional, precise. Like a mixing console or a well-worn record catalog.
- **Anti-references:** No glowy cyberpunk (no neon/cyan/purple gradients), no flat beige minimalism, no generic SaaS dashboards, no mobile-first consumer aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Density with clarity** — Pack information tightly but never obscure it.
2. **Music-first typography** — Editorial hierarchy, strong contrast, nods to liner notes and catalog indexes.
3. **Consequential actions deserve weight** — Destructive operations feel serious and deliberate, not alarming.
4. **Familiar darkness** — Warm dark theme; orange for primary actions, sage green for source/success states.
5. **No decorative chrome** — Every visual element must earn its presence.
